# Project guide

Infer the smallest useful rigor level from evidence; ask only when the answer changes required work:

- hobby/prototype: prevent data loss, secrets and severe security/legal mistakes; avoid ceremony;
- professional MVP: add repeatable tests, ownership, basic operations, privacy and license checks;
- production: require observability, capacity/failure behavior, rollback or tested restore, maintained records and the team's chosen change discipline;
- critical/regulatory: require explicit controls, approvals, traceability and recovery evidence.

Scale, tenancy, sensitive data, money, distribution and legal exposure can raise rigor.

Identify whether the user needs a new project, adoption, synchronization, active feature/fix, review, release or operational response. Recover the smallest unfinished step from evidence, state that action and why it matters, then offer at most one follow-up.

For a greeting or vague help request, perform no repository-wide scan. Briefly mention `project guide`, `feature start`, `fix start` and `help` in the user's language.
